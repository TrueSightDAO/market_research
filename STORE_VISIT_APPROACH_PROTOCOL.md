# Store Visit Approach Protocol
## Individual Store Visit Guide for Market Research

Complete step-by-step protocol for approaching stores during in-person visits. Based on successful partnerships and lessons learned from East West Bookshop and Love of Ganesha.

---

## üéØ Pre-Visit Preparation (Before You Arrive)

### 1. Research the Store (15-30 minutes before visit)

**Essential Research:**
- [ ] Check store hours (Google Maps, Yelp, website)
- [ ] Review store's website/social media (understand their vibe, products, values)
- [ ] Note store owner/manager name (if available on website)
- [ ] Identify parking options nearby
- [ ] Review their product mix (verify they're not crystal-only or candle-only)
- [ ] Check if they have food permit (if required in that city)

**Quick Check:**
- [ ] Verify address on Google Street View
- [ ] Check recent reviews (understand customer base)
- [ ] Note any special events or classes they offer

### 2. Prepare Your Materials

**Bring With You:**
- [ ] **Product sample** (small amount of cacao in sealed container)
- [ ] **Business cards** (or contact info card)
- [ ] **Product information sheet** (one-page: product specs, sourcing, values)
- [ ] **Pricing sheet** (wholesale prices, markup calculations)
- [ ] **Phone** (for photos, notes, Google Maps)
- [ ] **Notebook** (for immediate notes after visit)
- [ ] **Product photos** (on phone - show bags, packaging, farm photos)

**Optional but Helpful:**
- [ ] Small sample packets (for them to try)
- [ ] Printed testimonials or partner list
- [ ] Calculator (for quick markup calculations)

### 3. Mental Preparation

**Before Entering:**
- [ ] Review your key talking points (30 seconds)
- [ ] Prepare your opening line
- [ ] Know your pricing structure (wholesale prices, markup options)
- [ ] Be ready to offer both consignment and purchase
- [ ] Set intention: Create connection, not just sell

---

## üö™ Entering the Store

### Step 1: Initial Entry (First 30 seconds)

**Body Language:**
- ‚úÖ Confident, friendly demeanor
- ‚úÖ Make eye contact with staff
- ‚úÖ Smile naturally
- ‚úÖ Don't rush - take in the store atmosphere

**What to Do:**
1. **Enter naturally** - browse for 30-60 seconds
2. **Observe the space** - products, vibe, customer flow
3. **Identify the decision-maker** - owner, manager, or staff member
4. **Look for product placement opportunities** - where would cacao fit?

**What NOT to Do:**
- ‚ùå Don't immediately ask for the owner
- ‚ùå Don't pull out samples immediately
- ‚ùå Don't interrupt staff helping customers

## üí¨ Opening Approach (The First 2 Minutes)

### Option A: Natural Conversation Start (Recommended)

**When staff greets you:**
```
Staff: "Hi, can I help you find something?"
You: "Hi! I'm actually here to learn about your store. I love the [crystals/wellness/books] 
     you have. Do you have a moment to talk about a potential product partnership?"
```

**If browsing:**
```
You (to staff): "Hi, I'm [Name] from Agroverse. We make ceremonial cacao from regenerative 
               farms in Brazil. I'm wondering if you might be interested in carrying our 
               products. Is the owner or manager available?"
```

### Option B: Direct Approach (If store is quiet)

```
You: "Hi, I'm [Name] from Agroverse. We source ceremonial cacao directly from regenerative 
     farms in Brazil. I'd love to show you what we have and see if it might be a good fit 
     for your store. Is there a good time to talk?"
```

### Key Principles:
- **Be respectful of their time** - "Do you have a moment?"
- **Show genuine interest** - Compliment their store
- **State your purpose clearly** - Product partnership
- **Be flexible** - If they're busy, offer to come back

## üé§ The Pitch (2-5 minutes)

### Core Message Structure

#### 1. **Introduction (30 seconds)**
```
"Hi, I'm [Name] from Agroverse. We make 100% ceremonial cacao sourced directly from 
regenerative farms in Brazil. We work directly with farmers like Oscar, Paulo, and Vivi 
to support regenerative agriculture and community."
```

#### 2. **Why It Fits Their Store (30-60 seconds)**
**For Spiritual/Metaphysical Shops:**
```
"I noticed you have [crystals/herbs/wellness items]. Ceremonial cacao fits perfectly with 
your community because it's used in sacred circles, intention-setting, and group rituals. 
It's a life-force enhancer that complements your spiritual tools."
```

**For Wellness Centers:**
```
"Your focus on [yoga/breathwork/healing] aligns with ceremonial cacao's use in wellness 
practices. It's a heart-opening, ceremonial-grade product that supports transformation 
and connection."
```

**For Boutique Chocolatiers:**
```
"As a boutique that values ethical sourcing, you'll appreciate that we work directly with 
farmers, use regenerative farming practices, and plant trees with every purchase. It's 
100% cacao - no additives, single-origin from Brazil."
```

#### 3. **Show Product (1-2 minutes)**
- Show product sample (if you brought one)
- Show photos on phone (packaging, farm, process)
- Explain the product: "100% ceremonial cacao, ground from whole beans, regenerative farming"

#### 4. **Values Alignment (30 seconds)**
```
"What makes us unique is our direct farmer relationships and regenerative practices. 
We plant trees with every purchase, support Amazon rainforest regeneration, and work 
with farmers who practice regenerative agriculture."
```

### Listen for Their Response

**Watch for:**
- Interest level (are they asking questions?)
- Concerns (pricing? fit? logistics?)
- Decision-making process (do they need to think? consult partner?)
- Timing (when do they make buying decisions?)

## üí∞ Pricing Discussion (Critical Section)

### When They Ask About Pricing

#### Step 1: Ask About Their Needs First
```
"What markup do you typically need? We can structure pricing to accommodate your 
requirements."
```

#### Step 2: Offer Both Options
```
"We offer two options:
1. **Consignment** - You pay when items sell, no upfront cost
2. **Outright Purchase** - You buy wholesale, set your own retail price"
```

#### Step 3: Calculate Together (If They Need Specific Markup)

**If they need 100% markup:**
```
"If you want to retail at $12, and you need 100% markup, that means:
- Retail: $12
- Wholesale: $6 (you double your money)
- Our cost allows for this structure"
```

**If they need 50% markup:**
```
"If you want to retail at $12, and you need 50% markup, that means:
- Retail: $12
- Wholesale: $8 (you add 50% margin)
- More room in the pricing structure"
```

**Use your calculator** - Show them the math transparently

#### Step 4: Be Flexible
```
"We're flexible on pricing structure. What works best for your business model?"
```

### Common Pricing Objections & Responses

**Objection: "Your wholesale price is too high"**
- Response: "What retail price are you comfortable with? Let's work backwards from that."
- Calculate: Show them different markup scenarios
- Offer: Volume discounts for larger orders

**Objection: "I don't like consignment"**
- Response: "No problem - we can do outright purchase. What quantity would you want to start with?"
- Reference: "Love of Ganesha in SF prefers purchase too - it works great for them."

**Objection: "I need to think about it"**
- Response: "Absolutely - here's my card and product info. When would be a good time to follow up?"
- Leave materials: Product sheet, pricing, contact info

## üéØ Handling Different Responses

### Scenario 1: Immediate Interest ‚úÖ

**Signs:**
- Asking detailed questions
- Wanting to see product
- Discussing placement in store
- Talking about quantities

**Action:**
1. Get their contact info
2. Offer to send follow-up materials
3. Schedule next step (sample delivery, formal proposal)
4. **Take notes immediately** after leaving

### Scenario 2: Needs Time to Think ü§î

**Signs:**
- "I need to think about it"
- "Let me check with my partner"
- "We're not making buying decisions right now"

**Action:**
1. **Don't push** - Respect their process
2. Leave materials (card, product sheet, pricing)
3. Ask: "When would be a good time to follow up?"
4. Offer: "I can send you more information via email"
5. **Note follow-up date** in your system

### Scenario 3: Not a Good Fit ‚ùå

**Signs:**
- "We don't carry food products"
- "We only sell [single product type]"
- "Not interested" (clear rejection)

**Action:**
1. **Accept gracefully** - "I understand, thank you for your time"
2. Ask: "Do you know any other shops that might be interested?"
3. Leave card anyway (they might refer you)
4. **Update status** in Google Sheet: "Rejected - [reason]"

### Scenario 4: They're Busy ‚è∞

**Signs:**
- Store is busy with customers
- Staff is rushed
- Not a good time

**Action:**
1. **Respect their time** - "I see you're busy"
2. Ask: "When would be a better time to come back?"
3. Leave materials: "Here's my card and product info - I'll follow up"
4. **Schedule return visit** if they're interested

## üìù Post-Visit Actions (Critical - Do Immediately)

### Within 5 Minutes of Leaving Store

**1. Take Notes (Use phone or notebook):**
- [ ] Store name, address, contact info
- [ ] Person you spoke with (name, role)
- [ ] Their response (interested/thinking/rejected)
- [ ] Key points discussed (pricing, concerns, timeline)
- [ ] Follow-up needed (when, what)
- [ ] Any referrals they gave

**2. Update Google Sheet:**
- [ ] Mark status: "Visited" or "Contacted"
- [ ] Add visit date
- [ ] Add outcome notes
- [ ] Add sales process notes (what worked, what didn't)
- [ ] Set follow-up date if needed

**3. Take Photos (If helpful):**
- [ ] Storefront (for reference)
- [ ] Product placement ideas (where cacao would fit)
- [ ] Business card (if they gave one)

### Follow-Up Actions

**If They're Interested:**
- [ ] Send email within 24 hours (thank them, recap, next steps)
- [ ] Send product information and pricing
- [ ] Offer to deliver sample
- [ ] Schedule follow-up call/visit

**If They Need Time:**
- [ ] Send brief email (thank you, remind them of your conversation)
- [ ] Leave materials with them
- [ ] Set calendar reminder for follow-up
- [ ] Add to follow-up list

**If They Rejected:**
- [ ] Update status in sheet
- [ ] Note reason (for learning)
- [ ] Thank them for their time (brief email if appropriate)

## üö® Common Mistakes to Avoid

### ‚ùå Don't Do These:

1. **Don't oversell** - Let them show interest first
2. **Don't interrupt** - If they're helping customers, wait
3. **Don't push pricing** - If they're not interested, don't force it
4. **Don't forget to listen** - Their needs matter more than your pitch
5. **Don't skip follow-up** - Always update your sheet and follow up
6. **Don't take rejection personally** - It's business, not personal
7. **Don't forget to ask for referrals** - Even if they say no
8. **Don't show up unannounced during busy times** - Respect their schedule

### ‚úÖ Do These:

1. **Be respectful** - Of their time, space, customers
2. **Be genuine** - Show real interest in their store
3. **Be flexible** - Adapt to their needs
4. **Be prepared** - Know your product, pricing, values
5. **Be persistent but not pushy** - Follow up appropriately
6. **Be transparent** - Honest about pricing, structure
7. **Be patient** - Sales take time, relationships matter
8. **Be grateful** - Thank them for their time, always

- Direct, clear approach
- Offered outright purchase (not just consignment)
- Transparent pricing
- Product fit their values perfectly

**Key Takeaway:** Some shops prefer purchase over consignment - always offer both

### East West Bookshop (Mountain View) - ‚ùå Rejected

**What Happened:**
- Shop was interested in product
- Issue was pricing structure (needed 100% markup, didn't like consignment)

- Always calculate markup upfront
- Offer purchase option if consignment is rejected
- Pricing structure is negotiable

## üí° Pro Tips

1. **First impressions matter** - Be confident, friendly, respectful
2. **Listen more than talk** - Understand their needs first
3. **Be flexible** - Every shop is different
4. **Follow up promptly** - Within 24 hours if they're interested
5. **Track everything** - Notes help you learn and improve
6. **Build relationships** - Even if they say no now, they might refer you
7. **Learn from each visit** - What worked? What didn't?
8. **Stay positive** - Rejections are part of the process
