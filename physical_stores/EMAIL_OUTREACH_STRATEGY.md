# Email Outreach Strategy
## Complete Guide for Partner Store Email Outreach

Email outreach is a powerful complement to in-person visits. Use it to reach stores before visits, follow up after visits, and connect with stores you can't visit in person.

---

## üéØ When to Use Email Outreach

### **Best Use Cases:**

1. **Pre-Visit Warm-Up** (Recommended)
   - Email stores 1-2 weeks before your visit
   - Introduce yourself and product
   - Offer to schedule a meeting when you're in town
   - **Benefit:** Higher success rate for in-person visits

2. **Post-Visit Follow-Up** (Critical)
   - Send within 24 hours of visit
   - Thank them, recap conversation
   - Provide requested materials
   - **Benefit:** Maintains momentum, shows professionalism

3. **Distant Stores** (High ROI)
   - Stores too far for in-person visit
   - Out-of-state opportunities
   - **Benefit:** Expands reach without travel costs

4. **Re-Engagement**
   - Stores that said "maybe later"
   - Follow-up after 3-6 months
   - **Benefit:** Reopens conversations

5. **Referral Follow-Up**
   - When stores refer you to others
   - Mention the referral source
   - **Benefit:** Higher response rate with warm intro

### **When NOT to Use Email:**

- ‚ùå First contact for local stores (in-person is better)
- ‚ùå If they explicitly said "no email"
- ‚ùå Mass blast without personalization
- ‚ùå Reaching out too frequently (spam territory)

## üìä Email Best Practices

### **1. Subject Lines**

**Good:**
- ‚úÖ Personal and specific: "Partnership opportunity: [Store Name]"
- ‚úÖ Clear value: "Ceremonial cacao for spiritual shops"
- ‚úÖ Referral: "[Name] suggested I reach out"
- ‚úÖ Question: "Would [Store Name] be interested in ceremonial cacao?"

**Avoid:**
- ‚ùå Generic: "Partnership opportunity" (too vague)
- ‚ùå Spammy: "LIMITED TIME OFFER" (sounds like spam)
- ‚ùå Too long: Over 50 characters gets cut off
- ‚ùå All caps: Looks unprofessional

### **2. Personalization**

**Always Personalize:**
- [ ] Store name
- [ ] Owner/manager name (if you know it)
- [ ] Specific thing about their store
- [ ] Their values/products
- [ ] How you found them

**Research Before Sending:**
- Check their website
- Review their social media
- Note their product mix
- Understand their values

### **3. Timing**

**Best Days to Send:**
- Tuesday-Thursday (highest open rates)
- Avoid Monday (busy) and Friday (weekend mode)

**Best Times:**
- 9-11 AM (morning check)
- 2-3 PM (after lunch)
- Avoid early morning (before 8 AM) and late evening (after 6 PM)

**Time Zone:**
- Send in their time zone, not yours

### **4. Length**

**Keep It Short:**
- 100-200 words for initial outreach
- 50-100 words for follow-ups
- Get to the point quickly
- Use bullet points for clarity

**People are busy** - Respect their time

### **5. Clear Call-to-Action (CTA)**

**Good CTAs:**
- "Would you be open to a 15-minute call?"
- "Would [Date/Time] work for a brief meeting?"
- "I'd love to send you a sample - would that work?"
- "Can I send you more information?"

**Avoid:**
- "Let me know if you're interested" (too vague)
- "Buy now!" (too pushy)
- Multiple CTAs (confusing)

## üîÑ Follow-Up Sequences

### **Sequence 1: Cold Outreach (No Response)**

**Email 1:** Initial outreach (Day 0)
**Email 2:** Gentle follow-up (Day 7)
   - Subject: "Re: [Original subject]"
   - Brief: "Just wanted to make sure this didn't get buried in your inbox..."
   - Keep it short and friendly

**Email 3:** Final follow-up (Day 14)
   - Subject: "Last try: [Store Name] partnership"
   - Brief: "I know you're busy - just wanted to try one more time..."
   - Accept "no" gracefully

**After 3 emails:** Stop. Move on.

### **Sequence 2: Post-Visit Follow-Up**

**Email 1:** Thank you + materials (Day 0 - within 24 hours)
**Email 2:** Check-in (Day 7-10)
   - "Just checking in - do you have any questions?"
   - Keep it brief
**Email 3:** Final follow-up (Day 14-21)
   - "I wanted to follow up one more time..."
   - Accept if they're not interested

**If They Respond:** Engage in conversation, don't stick to schedule

### **Sequence 3: Re-Engagement**

**Email 1:** Reconnect (Month 3-6)
**Email 2:** Final check (Month 6-9)
**After 2 emails:** Stop. They're not interested.

## üö® Common Email Mistakes

### **‚ùå Don't Do These:**

1. **Mass Emails Without Personalization**
   - Generic "Dear Store Owner"
   - No mention of their store
   - Copy-paste without changes

2. **Too Long**
   - Walls of text
   - Multiple paragraphs
   - Overwhelming information

3. **Too Pushy**
   - Multiple follow-ups too quickly
   - Aggressive language
   - "Last chance" messaging

4. **Poor Timing**
   - Sending at wrong times
   - Following up too soon
   - Ignoring their time zone

5. **No Clear Next Step**
   - Vague call-to-action
   - Multiple CTAs
   - Unclear what you want

6. **Spam Triggers**
   - ALL CAPS
   - Excessive exclamation points!!!
   - Spam words (free, guaranteed, etc.)
   - Too many links

### **‚úÖ Do These:**

1. **Personalize Every Email**
   - Research their store
   - Mention specific things
   - Show you care

2. **Keep It Brief**
   - 100-200 words max
   - Get to the point
   - Use bullet points

3. **Be Respectful**
   - Respect their time
   - Don't push if they say no
   - Accept "maybe later"

4. **Clear Next Step**
   - One clear CTA
   - Make it easy to respond
   - Offer options (call/email)

5. **Professional Tone**
   - Friendly but professional
   - Not too casual
   - Not too formal

6. **Test Before Sending**
   - Proofread
   - Check links
   - Test on mobile

## üí° Advanced Strategies

### **1. A/B Testing**

**Test:**
- Subject lines
- Email length
- CTA placement
- Personalization level

**How:**
- Send 2 versions to similar stores
- Track response rates
- Use what works

### **2. Segmentation**

**Group Stores By:**
- Type (spiritual/wellness/boutique)
- Size (large/small)
- Location (local/distant)
- Response level (interested/thinking/no)

**Customize:**
- Different templates for each group
- Different messaging
- Different timing

### **3. Automation (If Using Email Tool)**

**Set Up:**
- Auto-follow-ups (if no response)
- Reminders for manual follow-ups
- Templates for different scenarios

**Tools to Consider:**
- Mailchimp (for larger lists)
- SendGrid (for transactional)
- Gmail with templates (simple, free)

### **4. Integration with Store Visits**

**Workflow:**
1. Email stores 1-2 weeks before visit
2. Visit stores that responded
3. Follow up via email after visit
4. Track everything in Google Sheets

**Benefit:** Higher success rate, better preparation
